Description

The purpose of this project was to explore how to convey a simple nursery rhyme, The Farmer in the Dell, through exploring the relationships between typography and graphic element.

Tools

Adobe Illustrator, Adobe InDesign

Approach

The design process began with figuring out how to convey the idea of connection. The story acts as almost on long chain of reactions so I wanted to visually represent linkages throughout the book. The first thing that came to mind was something resembling a family tree. Though it carried out the idea well, design wise I personally was not a fan. Next I thought of how I could use text to connect the words. Again, the idea came across however the hierarchy was lost as the way of reading the text began to get confusing. I began to think of not going the literal meaning route for visual representation and this opened up more possibilities for me. I didn’t have any false constraints and I could experiment more with ideas and graphic compositions.

Challenges

As more and more connections were being created, it became more difficult to distinguish one character’s design from another. As a result I wanted to explore the implementation of colour. This colour clarified and gave more personality to each entity. I extended this use of colour into the overall art direction and used it as a means to unify the entire publication as a whole.

Conclusion

This project allowed me to explore abstract means of graphic design. While I am perfectly comfortable designing within a grid system and with vertical and horizontal lines, this exploration allowed me to explore and begin practicing abstract art.
